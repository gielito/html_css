Launch of competition for Temporary Agents confirmed


In the past months the REA has been exploring the feasibility of obtaining support from EPSO to organise a selection to create a Reserve list of approximately 60 Research Programme Officers (AD5). EPSO has agreed to take on board the organisation of our competition and will design the procedure to start early 2018, with the aim of having the reserve list available by the second quarter of 2018.  The competition will be open also to Icelandic and Norwegian nationals and will follow the EPSO’s language rules. The competition may be jointly organised with other Executive Agencies. Further information such as dates and procedures will be clarified in the coming weeks.
Facing a difficult situation in life? !


The Psycho-social service of the Commission provides counselling services free of charge to help you face difficult situations such as emotional stress, financial issues, or any other problem in your working environment or family life. The team consists of social workers, psychologists, a psychiatrist, a social nurse and a budget counsellor. The consultations are confidential and appointments can be made during working hours. "Seeking help is the first step towards overcoming your troubles," says the psycho-social team, and encourages you to "ask for advice before difficulties turn into severe problems."
REA External Communication Workplan has been adopted


The upcoming external communication activities will focus on three priorities: 1) Promote calls reaching a wider share of the scientific community; 2) Consolidate a service-oriented communication with participants and experts; 3) Support parent DGs in giving visibility to EU research via success stories and provide input to the policy-feedback loop. Some of the actions planned include the production of news alerts, articles and videos on funding opportunities and the draft of a Client Service Charter.
